For English-speaking patients in Tel Aviv

VEDICA is built for people who want Ayurveda explained clearly and practically. For English-speaking patients, the consultation creates space to describe symptoms, daily habits, medical background, stress, digestion, sleep and energy in a language that feels natural and precise.

The clinic combines Ayurveda with traditional Persian and Iranian food knowledge. That means the conversation can include doshas, digestion, daily rhythm, herbs, spices, infusions, food habits, Inbal's Persian-Iranian heritage and the way the body responds to stress, seasons and lifestyle.

This is complementary care. It is not a substitute for diagnosis, medication, mental health care, fertility treatment or ongoing medical follow-up. When relevant, the plan is built to sit alongside conventional care.

FAQ

Are consultations available in English?

Yes. Consultations at VEDICA can be held in English or Hebrew, including for native English-speaking patients, expats, visitors and international residents in Tel Aviv.

What happens in the first Ayurvedic consultation?

The first consultation is usually about two hours. It includes a detailed conversation about digestion, sleep, energy, stress, habits and health background, alongside traditional pulse diagnosis. From there, Inbal builds a personalized plan with nutrition, herbs, infusions and practical daily routine guidance.

Can Ayurveda support digestion, stress, sleep or fertility?

Ayurvedic support at VEDICA is complementary and does not replace medical care. It can focus on nutrition, daily routine, stress support, sleep habits, herbs and personal guidance alongside conventional medical follow-up when needed.

Who is the English-speaking clinic a good fit for?

The clinic can be a good fit for English-speaking patients, expats, olim, international residents and visitors staying in Tel Aviv who want private Ayurveda and Persian medicine guidance in English.

What should I prepare before the first consultation?

It is helpful to bring or mention current medications, supplements, diagnoses, recent blood tests or medical documents if relevant, fertility or cycle context if relevant, and the main symptoms or goals you want to focus on.

Is the clinic suitable for English-speaking patients living in Tel Aviv?

Yes. The clinic is located at Arlozorov 82 in Tel Aviv and can support English-speaking patients who want Ayurveda and Persian medicine explained clearly in English.

Is VEDICA an Ayurvedic pharmacy?

No. VEDICA is a private Ayurveda consultation clinic, not an Ayurvedic pharmacy or walk-in herb shop. Herbs, infusions and spices are discussed as part of a personalized consultation and are adjusted to the person's health background, medications, digestion and routine.
